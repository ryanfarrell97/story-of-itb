Following my meeting with Roisin Long, a staff member working in the NLN in the A Block, I found out a few very interesting things.
The NLN was first brought into ITB in 2003 and has been a part of the college for 15 years
They are an educational psychological support service that offers supports in academics, wellbeing, social, organisation and there's a recognised overlap between academic ability and personal wellbeing.
Most students will use it for needs assessments that are useful for exam accomodations, CA project help and other specific supports if necessary.
A survey was carried out in 2017-18 that found that 131 students had registered with the service, 400+ appointments with the NLN were offered last year and that cards helped student engagement with the service.
The most availed of services they offer are needs assessments, study skills, essay writing, stress management and help with adjusting to life at 3rd level.
The purpose of the NLN's drop in service is to remove that formal barrier to help students feel at ease, and being a support service, they will try to help and support students in reaching their potential.

From the 2017/18 survey
72% of students felt the NLN supports helped improve their grades.
Of the students who reported having anxiety issues, 80% said that going to the NLN helped manage it.
15 students reported that the NLN changed their minds about dropping out of college.
97% of the students who went to the NLN would recommend them to a classmate.
The supports most valued by students were academic supports and having someone to talk to about the overall college experience.
